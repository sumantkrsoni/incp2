About the Workshop

Finite Element Method (FEM) is the most powerful method for the analysis of engineering problems like Multiphysics problems. It is capable of handling complicated geometry domains, a variety of boundary conditions, non-linearity, and coupled phenomena that are common in real-life problems.

The workshop includes invited talks, technical sessions, and hands-on training to equip participants with practical tools and insights. It will highlight novel algorithms, innovative frameworks, and real-world applications in various domains such as engineering, sustainability, artificial intelligence, modelling, and numerical methods to discuss the latest developments in transport phenomena and coupled problems in porous media.

Indo-Nor-Energy Project (INCP)

The Indo-Nor-Energy project (Indo-Norwegian Cooperation Programme 2024) links IIT Jammu, IIT Delhi, CUSB Gaya, and the University of Bergen to advance energy storage research. Focused on master’s and PhD students, it covers batteries, fuel cells, hydrogen, and geothermal solutions, supported by digital platforms and MOOCs.

With an interdisciplinary approach spanning energy, mathematics, medical sciences, and information security, it explores bio-inspired systems and microfluidic fuel cells. Combining modeling, computation, and experimentation, the project fosters critical thinking and broadens international exposure through events engaging neighboring universities.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding of FEM mathematical foundations.
  • Ability to implement basic FEM solvers.
  • Knowledge of error analysis techniques.
  • Introduction to PINNs (Physics-Informed Neural Networks) for PDEs.
  • Practical experience with MATLAB/Python implementation.

Topics to be Covered

  • Basics of functional analysis for FEM
  • Sobolev spaces and variational formulation
  • Finite element method for elliptic problems
  • Error analysis and adaptive methods
  • Implementation of FEM in MATLAB
  • Introduction to Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)
